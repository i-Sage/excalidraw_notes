From our previous studies, we found that small signal voltage gain and other
characteristics of BJTs and MOSFETs circuit amplifiers are functions of, for
example, the bipolar current gain and MOSFET conduction parameter. In general,
these transistor parameters vary with temperature and have a range of values
for a given transistor type.

These parameter variations mean that the Q-Point, voltage gain, and other
circuit parameters can vary from one circuit to another and can even be a 
function of temperature.

Transistor circuit parameters can essentially be made independent of the
individual transistor parameters by using FEEDBACK. ^RTnAJLSi

FEEDBACK AND STABILITY ^6SWBc9Qv

The feedback process takes a portion of the output signal and returns it to 
the input to become part of the input excitation.

OUR GOAL TODAY IS TO PROPERLY AND FORMALLY INTRODUCE OURSELVES TO THE MAGIC
THAT IS FEEDBACK, MORE SPECIFICALLY, THE BLACK MAGIC THAT IS NEGATIVE FEEDBACK. ^zicoTJeR

Starting off with the basics, feedback can either be positive or negative.
In NEGATIVE FEEDBACK, a portion of the output signal is subtracted from the
input signal. In POSITIVE FEEDBACK, a portion of the output signal is added
to the input signal.

NEGATIVE FEEDBACK for example tends to maintain a constant value of amplified
voltage against variations in transistor parameters, supply voltages and most
importantly temperature.

POSITIVE FEEDBACK on the other hand is used in the design of oscillators and 
in a number of other applications. ^jWkiwWzt

ADVANTAGES AND DISADVANTAGES OF NEGATIVE FEEDBACK ^5iaJd5T8

ADVANTAGES:
1.GAIN SENSITIVITY: Variations in the circuit transfer function (gain) as a
result of changes in transistor parameters are reduced by feedback. This
reduction in sensitivity is one of the most attractive features of negative
feedback.
2.BANDWIDTH EXTENSION: The bandwidth of a circuit that incorporates negative
feedback is larger than that of the basic amplifier.
3.NOISE SENSITIVITY: Negative feedback MAY increase the signal-to-noise ratio
(SNR) if noise is generated within the feedback loop.
4.REDUCTION OF NONLINEAR DISTORTION: Since transistors have non-linear
characteristics, distortion may appear in the output signal, especially at
larger signal levels. Negative feedback reduces this distortion.
5.CONTROL OF IMPEDANCE LEVELS: The input and output impedances can be 
increased or decreased with the proper type of negative feedback circuit.

DISADVANTAGES:
1.CIRCUIT GAIN: The overall amplifier gain, with negative feedback is reduced
compared to the basic amplifier used in the circuit.
2.STABILITY: There is a possibility that the feedback circuit may become
unstable (oscillate) at high frequencies. ^6IoTqpaT

BASIC FEEDBACK CONCEPTS ^PnVfmuXf

The above circuit diagrams shows us the basic configuration of a feedback amplifier.
The circuit contains a basic amplifier with an open loop gain A and a feedback circuit
that samples the output signal and produces a feedback signal.

The feedback signal is then subtracted from the input source signal, which produces
an error signal. The error signal is the input to the basic amplifier and is the
signal that is amplified to produce the output signal. The subtraction property
the negative feedback.  ^GBR3gyNH
